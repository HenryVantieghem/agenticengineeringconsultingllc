04 / Lead Scoring Algorithm

Weighted Scoring: 0–100

Every lead is scored across 7 dimensions. Only HOT (80+) and WARM (60+) leads enter the composition pipeline. Based on ML lead scoring research from Frontiers in AI.

Trigger Events x0.20
Hiring signals, new locations, bad reviews, competitor activity, seasonal patterns. Recent changes = buying window.
Review Score x0.15
4.0+ stars = established quality. Below 3.0 = too many problems to take on new tools.
Website Quality x0.15
Modern site = invests in growth. Stale WordPress = needs help but may be receptive.
Tech Stack Gap x0.15
No CRM / no marketing automation = prime candidate. Already using Salesforce = harder sell.
Decision Maker Found x0.15
Can we reach the owner/partner directly? info@ = low confidence. firstname@ = high confidence.
Review Volume x0.10
20+ reviews = established business with revenue. Under 5 = too early stage.
Social Presence x0.10
Active Facebook/Instagram = growth-minded. Zero social = may not value digital.

Qualification Tiers

HOT 80–100 Reach out immediately. High conversion probability. Strong trigger + verified decision maker.
WARM 60–79 Solid prospect. Include in daily batch. Good data quality, moderate trigger signals.
COOL 40–59 Include if batch needs volume. Missing some data points. Lower confidence.
SKIP 0–39 Not worth the email. Too small, too early, no contact info, or no buying signals.

ICP Profile

Revenue: $500K–$5M/yr
Team: 2–20 employees
Location: Mid-size US cities
Digital: Has website, NO CRM
Reviews: 3.5–4.5 stars, 20+
Signals: Hiring, active social
05 / Marketing Psychology

The LeadDrop Persuasion Framework

Every element of our outreach is engineered from peer-reviewed behavioral science: Cialdini, Kahneman, and data from 85,000+ emails.

01
Cialdini — Principle #1
Reciprocity

Give 5 free, personalized, genuinely valuable leads BEFORE asking for anything. Personalized + unexpected = strongest reciprocity trigger. 73% of trial users convert when given genuine free value.

Implementation: The free leads ARE the demo, the pitch, and the trust-builder — all in one email.
02
Attention Science
Pattern Interrupt

Most cold emails look identical. No "I hope this finds you well." Lead with value immediately. Subject lines: question-based, 4–7 words, specific to their business.

Subject: "5 people looking for a dentist in Auburn right now" — 46% open rate format.
03
Kahneman & Tversky
Loss Aversion

Loss framing is 2x more motivating than gain framing. Frame as what they are LOSING, not what they could gain. "Competitors will get these leads" beats "you could get new patients."

"These 5 leads will go to your competitors if you don't reach out this week."
04
Cialdini — Principle #3
Social Proof

Industry-specific results carry 3x more weight than generic claims. "We helped a dental practice in Atlanta find 20 new patients" beats "we help businesses grow."

Include competitor names, specific numbers, and industry-matched case studies.
05
UserGems + Boomerang Studies
Trigger Event Timing

Trigger-based outreach achieves 20–35% response vs. 5–10% for standard cold email. Timing the outreach to a hiring signal, bad review, or new competitor is a 4x multiplier.

Detect: hiring, funding, bad reviews, new competition, seasonal peaks. Act within the buying window.
06
85,000-Email Study Data
Optimal Mechanics

50–125 words, 6–8 sentences. Send Tuesday 6–9 AM recipient's time. Follow-up cadence: Day 3, Day 10, Day 17. Max 3 follow-ups. Batch size ≤50 contacts.

3–7–7 cadence captures 93% of total replies by Day 10. 4th follow-up = spam territory.
